Who?

A Mechatronics Graduate who enjoys all things Free Libre and Opensource. Enjoys the development of producing small embedded systems. A keen hobbyist who has spent time working on bringing AOSP to a series of Xperia devices.

What I Do

Takes an active role in developing AOSP for the Xperia line of devices and made steps to bringing CM10 to the Xperia X10 (first functional build) and then producing early Alpha builds for the S, Z and Z2 devices using Google's AOSP as a base.

Currently the Xperia devices maintainer for Paranoid Android (AOSPA). Working on getting more involved with frameworks development and recruitment of new developers for the project. Also takes a leading role in coordination of PR for the project. Builds can be found on the AOSPA website along with discussions and bug reports on XDA. We now have our own blog where you can find changelogs and further information from the team.

Typically working on small scale projects using Arduino, Raspberry Pi and Udoo. Most of these projects are for self teaching about certain aspects of technology whilst also trying to develop a more futuristic home setting.

Projects include a weather monitoring system with real time data logging, Home NAS, Home Surveillance System and Personal File Server.
